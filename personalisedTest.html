Eye Problems

Dry Eye Syndrome: A condition where the eyes do not produce enough tears or the tears evaporate too quickly, leading to dryness, irritation, and discomfort.

Conjunctivitis: Also known as pink eye, it involves inflammation of the conjunctiva, often caused by infections (bacterial or viral), allergies, or irritants.

Cataracts: Clouding of the eye's lens, which can lead to blurred vision and is often age-related.

Floaters and Flashers: Floaters are small spots or thread-like strands that drift through your field of vision, while flashers are brief bursts of light.

Stomach Problems

Acid Reflux: A burning sensation in the chest or throat caused by stomach acid rising into the esophagus. It often occurs after eating or lying down.

Nausea: An uneasy or queasy feeling in the stomach that often precedes vomiting. It can be caused by motion sickness, food poisoning, or digestive disturbances.

Gas and Bloating: The accumulation of gas in the stomach or intestines leading to a feeling of fullness, pressure, or swelling in the abdomen.

Constipation: Difficulty passing stools or infrequent bowel movements, often accompanied by hard, dry stools and straining during bowel movements.

Foot Symptoms and Diseases

Blisters: Fluid-filled sacs that form on the skin due to friction or pressure.

Calluses: Thickened skin areas that develop due to repeated friction or pressure, often on the soles or heels.

Corn: Small, thickened areas of skin, usually on the toes, caused by friction from poorly fitting shoes.

Athlete's Foot: A fungal infection that causes itching, redness, and peeling, typically between the toes.

Foot Odor: Unpleasant smell due to bacteria and fungi thriving in moist environments.
